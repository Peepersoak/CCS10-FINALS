HYDROELECTRIC ENERGY

Hydroelectric energy, also known as hydroelectric power or hydroelectricity, is a type of energy that uses the power of moving water to generate electricity, such as water running over a waterfall. For millennia, people have exploited this force. People in Greece employed flowing water to turn the wheel of their mill, which pounded wheat into flour over two thousand years ago.

How does hydroelectricity produce?

The kinetic energy of the water flowing down the dam is used to drive a turbine. The generator transfers the mechanical energy of the turbine into electricity. This electric energy is then passed through several transmission processes before reaching you.

WATER TURBINE

Water travels through a pipe, also known as a penstock, and then rotates the blades of a turbine, which turns a generator, which eventually creates energy. Most traditional hydroelectric facilities, including run-of-the-river and pumped storage systems, function in this manner.

ADVANTAGES OF HYDROELECTRIC ENERGY

  • Cheapest energy source
  • Very low emissions
  • Flood control

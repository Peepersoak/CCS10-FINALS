EXHAUST EMISSIONS


Several gases and particles are emitted as exhaust emissions when a car's engine is functioning. Particularly in densely populated places with significant levels of vehicular activity, these contaminants may worsen the quality of the air we breathe.

Catalytic Converter

Hazardous gases and other pollutants are present in the exhaust gas from an internal combustion engine, which a catalytic converter transforms by accelerating a redox reaction into less dangerous pollutants. In addition to internal combustion engines that run on gasoline, diesel, or even lean-burn fuel, catalytic converters are occasionally utilized with kerosene heaters and stoves.

Learn the Catalytic Converter

A muffler-like chamber filled with porous, heat-resistant materials that contain either a platinum or palladium coating serves as the basic building block of a catalytic converter. Catalysts are the name given to these chemicals. An element is a catalyst when it causes a reaction but experiences no change as a result of the reaction. This idea serves as the foundation for a catalytic converter. The carbon monoxide gas and hydrocarbons expelled by the engine will travel through the exhaust system until they reach the catalytic converter.

Despite being most frequently employed in automobile exhaust systems, catalytic converters are also used in:



Electrical Generators

Mining Equipment

Ships

Frequently Asked Questions

Catalytic converters are used in the majority of modern gasoline-powered vehicles, including cars, trucks, buses, trains, motorbikes, and airplanes.

90% of toxic emissions are converted into less harmful gases by the catalytic converter, lowering the amount of airborne pollutants that could be dangerous for both humans and the environment. A well maintained catalytic converter improves your car's efficiency, which has a direct impact on the condition and functionality of the entire thing.

To limit the emission of gaseous pollutants such carbon monoxide (CO), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and hydrocarbons, a catalytic converter is a component that attaches to the front of an automobile's exhaust system, close to the engine (HCs).

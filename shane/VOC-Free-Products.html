Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)

Numerous items we use on a daily basis, including as paints, cleansers, adhesives, sealants, furniture, and flooring, emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs), which can accumulate in enclosed spaces if sufficient ventilation is not provided. As a result of worries about the effects of these emissions, more consumer goods and building materials are being created and certified as low-emitting or even VOC-free. Many building codes demand or give credit for the use of low-emitting building materials and integrating low-emitting consumer products as part of an indoor air quality strategy since these developments are essential for reducing indoor air pollution.

Learn the Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)

High vapor pressure and little water solubility are characteristics of volatile organic molecules. In the creation of paints, medications, and refrigerants, numerous human-made compounds known as VOCs are utilized and created. Some solids or liquids release volatile organic compounds (VOCs) into the air as gases. There are many different chemicals that make up VOCs, some of which may be harmful to your health in the short- and long-term. Many VOCs are consistently present in higher concentrations indoors than they are outside (up to ten times higher).
